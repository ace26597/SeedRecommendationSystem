Corn

Planning

Maize crop is usually cultivated n India, during June to July , Sept to Oct and Jan to Feb seasons.

For seed production , sowing during Nov to Dec is suitable since seed maturity will not concede with the rainy season. With the availability of photononsensitive maize varieties, it is possible to grow maize throughout the year. Maize can be cultivated in three seasons.

Kharif crop

It is better to sow the Kharif crop at least fortnight before the rain starts to avoid problems during sowing so that the seedlings are well established before they get heavy rains. Early sowing also reduces weed problems, the crop matures earlier and field becomes free for next rabi crop.

Rabi crop

The optimum date of sowing is end of October to mid December and crop is raised under assured irrigation.

Spring crop

The crop is grown in Bihar, Maharastra and Tarai belt of UP and most optimum time of sowing is late January to end of February.

In the southern plains where there is no danger of frost, The first transplanting is done in December-January, Second June-July Third in September-October depending on the irrigation facilities available.

Irrigation

Water requirement of Kharif maize is 400-550 mm while that of Rabi maize is 450-600 mm. Even though maize roots can penetrate upto 120 cm., the density of roots is high only at 60 cm. First irrigation should be given upto 120 Cm, depth. Second irrigation after 10 to 15 days depending upon soil, temperature and wind velocity. Schedule irrigations at more frequent intervals in dry weather and less frequent intervals in humid weather.

Disease Management

Use of resistant varieties like DHM 103, Ganga Safed 2. Crop sanitation, crop rotations.

Avoiding water stress at flowering.

Seed treatment with Thiram or Captan 3g/kg seed.

To control Downy mildew remove the affected plants,spray Metalaxyl 72 WP @1kh per hectare, Mancozeb 1 kg per hectare 20 days after sowing. For leaf spot control spray Captan or Mancozeb kg per hectare if intensity is high.

Harvesting

The grain crop can be harvested when cob-sheath turns brownish, grains become hard and they do not contain more than 20 per cent moisture in them.

Plucking of cobs

The ears are removed from the standing plants and they are piled to open for twenty four hours and they are spread for drying in the sun. In this method stalks may be used as green fodder.

Stalk cutting

The plants are cut and piled up in the shade and the cobs are removed after two or three days of harvesting. The dried plants are used for haymaking.Maize grown for fodder are harvested at the milk to early dough stage. The earlier harvested crop usually yields less and is poor in protein content. For silage making late dough stage is preferred.
